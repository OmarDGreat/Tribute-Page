Kendrick Lamar

A visionary rapper and lyricist whose music transcends genres

Here's a timeline of Kendrick Lamar's life

  • 1987: Kendrick Lamar Duckworth is born in Compton, Californ, USA.
  • 2004: Kendrick releases his first mixtape titled "Youngest Head Nigga in Charge (Hub City Threat: Minor of the Year)" under the name K-Dot.
  • 2010: He releases another mixtape called "Overly Dedicated."
  • 2011: Kendrick gains significant recognition with the release of his independent album "Section.80."
  • 2012: Kendrick Lamar releases his critically acclaimed album "good kid, m.A.A.d city," which includes hit singles like "Swimming Pools (Drank)" and "Bitch, Don't Kill My Vibe."
  • 2015: He releases "To Pimp a Butterfly," another critically acclaimed album that addresses socl and political issues. This album includes tracks like "Alright" and "The Blacker the Berry."
  • 2017: His album DAMN is released, which also wins Grammys for Best Rap Album, and makes history as the first of its genre to earn a Pulitzer Prize.
  • 2019: Kendrick becomes a father. thethings.com
  • 2020: In August, Kendrick Lamar's company, pgLang, releases a short film titled "LAMA SQUAD," featuring Baby Keem.
  • 2021: In August, Kendrick Lamar announces that his next album will be his last with Top Dawg Entertainment, signaling the end of an era.
